Cheetah’s Homecoming
THE MURMUR OF
KUNO GRASSLANDS




Reference to context questions:



Context 1:



  1. Arjun presses his ear to the ground and listens carefully to the sounds of the grasslands to feel close to nature.

  2. Arjun knows about the cheetahs that once lived in Kuno but had disappeared long before he was born.



Context 2:



  1. Arjun is so excited that he cannot sleep and eagerly joins the crowd the next day to welcome the cheetahs.

  2. The cheetahs are brought to Kuno National Park by air and then carried from the airport in special vehicles.



Context 3:



  1. The crowd cheers loudly and watches with excitement and awe as the cheetahs arrive.

  2. Arjun is amazed by the cheetah’s beauty and feels thrilled, realizing it is even more magnificent than he had imagined.



Context 4:



  1. Arjun spends his free time watching the cheetahs, sketching them, and observing their behavior closely.

  2. Arjun keeps a notebook in which he sketches and writes notes about the cheetahs’ activities.



Context 5:



  1. A park ranger provides the information about the cheetah named Aasha.

  2. The name ‘Aasha’ means ‘hope’ in Hindi. It symbolizes hope for the successful return of cheetahs to India.



Context 6:



  1. The cheetahs are being released from their enclosures into the wider Kuno National Park after their acclimatization period.

  2. Arjun and his classmates are invited to witness the release of the cheetahs from a safe distance, and they watch in silent excitement and awe.



Answer of the Questions:-



  1. Bringing cheetahs back to Kuno National Park is very important because it helps restore India’s wildlife. Cheetahs had disappeared from India long ago, and their return shows hope for saving and protecting animals that were lost.

  2. At first, Arjun only loves listening to the sounds of nature. Later, he becomes more involved and curious. He watches the cheetahs, learns about them, and starts caring deeply for wildlife.

  3. The cheetahs might find it hard to get used to the new land, weather, and food. They also have to learn to live safely among other animals and humans in Kuno.

  4. The story shows that protecting animals and nature is very important. If people care for wildlife, even lost species like cheetahs can return and live freely again.

  5. The local people help by supporting the project, protecting the cheetahs, and spreading awareness about saving animals and nature.

  6. When Arjun learns about Aasha, the cheetah whose name means “hope,” he feels inspired. He understands that every animal is special and that humans must protect them.

  7. Cheetahs help keep nature balanced. They control the number of smaller animals and help the grasslands stay healthy. Their return makes the ecosystem stronger.

  8. The story mixes old and new ideas — it talks about India’s lost cheetahs (tradition) and also about the modern scientific project to bring them back (modernity)
