Cultural Connections
SMART WAYS




Reference to context questions:



Context 1:



  1. The poet means that both ancient stories and modern traditions carry wisdom and teach us valuable lessons.


  3. The poet includes both to show that wisdom is found in every generation — the past teaches us lessons while the new brings creativity and growth.



Context 2:



  1. The poet paints a lively and joyful image of festivals filled with colors, lights, and celebration.

  2. It refers to the creativity, talent, and imagination of different cultures expressed through their festivals.

  3. Festivals bring people together, promote understanding and appreciation of each other’s traditions, and strengthen unity.



Context 3:



  1. It means the valuable collection of cultural knowledge, traditions, and wisdom passed down through generations.

  2. Sharing cultural wisdom helps people understand and respect one another, showing kindness and care for others.

  3. Because learning and sharing traditions make people wiser and more understanding — that’s the “smart” way to live together peacefully.



Context 4:



  1. Family members, especially elders, keep traditions alive by passing them on through songs, stories, and food.

  2. The poet describes them as colorful and lively — a “vibrant scene” full of joy and meaning.

  3. It suggests that traditions make life beautiful, colorful, and full of diversity.



Answer of the Questions:-



  1. The poem shows that both old and new customs are valuable. Old tales give wisdom, and new traditions bring creativity. Together they make our lives rich and meaningful.

  2. The poet uses images like “festivals burst with colour and light” and “traditions paint a vibrant scene” to show the variety and beauty of different cultures.

  3. Festivals help people from different places come together, celebrate, and understand each other’s traditions, strengthening unity and cultural bonds.

  4. The poem suggests we should learn with open minds and hearts. This is important because it helps us grow wiser, kinder, and more understanding.

  5. The “bright stars” represent valuable lessons from history and culture that continue to shine and guide us even today.

  6. The poem shows that learning about other cultures helps us grow personally and become wiser. Lines like “With open minds, we learn and grow” express that cultural understanding makes us better human beings.
