Reference to context questions



Context 1:



  1. The poet describes the rain as soft and gentle, falling quietly every day.

  2. The rain is compared to a gentle ocean spray.

  3. These lines create a calm, peaceful, and refreshing mood.



Context 2:



  1. The poet describes each raindrop as a sparkling gem.

  2. The rain covers grass and leaves with shining drops, making them fresh and beautiful.

  3. It creates the image of shining raindrops decorating plants like jewels.



Context 3:



  1. Trees are personified as “thirsty” beings who “drink” rain and “dance” happily.

  2. The trees become refreshed, and their branches seem to dance with joy.

  3. It shows that rain gives life and energy to trees, making them strong and lively.



Context 4:



  1. The wilted flowers lift their heads and become fresh again.

  2. The Earth is soaking up the raindrops.

  3. It shows that rain restores life to both flowers and the Earth, bringing freshness and strength.



Context 5:



  1. The birds are sitting on window sills.

  2. They fluff their feathers to keep warm.

  3. It shows that rain affects animals too, and they find ways to protect themselves from cold and wetness.



Context 6:



  1. “They” refers to the birds.

  2. The birds shake off water, fly into the sky, and look for the Sun as clouds clear.

  3. It suggests that after the rain, the sky clears, the Sun appears, and life becomes active and lively again.



Answer of the Questions:



  1. The poem shows rain as gentle and refreshing, helping all parts of nature.

    • It softens the Earth like a mist.

    • It refreshes vegetation by coating leaves and grass with sparkling drops.

    • Trees become lively and dance when they get rain.

    • Wilted flowers bloom again, and the Earth soaks up the rainwater.


    Even animals and birds respond to rain by finding shelter and later enjoying the Sun.

    • Thus, the rain is portrayed as a friend who brings life, freshness, and energy to the whole of nature.

  2. Rain refreshes the earth, revives plants, and fills rivers and ponds. It brings greenery, coolness in the air, and life to animals, birds, and humans.

  3. The poet uses imagery of shining raindrops, refreshed leaves, and blooming flowers. Words like “sparkling gem” and the sound of raindrops create a lively picture in the reader’s mind.

  4. Plants stretch, bloom, and look fresh after rain, while animals and birds feel joyful and active. Both are revived, but plants show it by growth, while animals show it by movement and sound.

  5. The rain is shown as a friend of nature, helping plants, animals, and humans equally. It supports life, removes heat and dryness, and connects all living beings.

  6. The poem shows how rain renews life—dry soil becomes fertile, plants grow fresh, and the whole environment is purified and transformed.

  7. The mood is joyful, refreshing, and hopeful. The poet achieves this by using bright imagery, describing the beauty of raindrops, and showing how nature celebrates rainfall.

  8. The poem uses a regular rhyme scheme of ABAB and short stanzas. This rhythm mimics the pitter-patter of rain and makes the poem musical and pleasing to read.
